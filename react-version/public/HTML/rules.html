RULES & REGULATIONS

1. General Conduct and Fair Play

Do: All participants must adhere to the principles of fair play, respect, and good sportsmanship. Players are expected to treat each other, referees, and organizers with respect, maintaining a positive environment.

Don’t: Cheating, hacking, or using any unauthorized third-party software is strictly prohibited. Any form of toxicity, harassment, or unsportsmanlike behavior will result in immediate disqualification.

2. Match Setup and Equipment

Do: Players are responsible for ensuring their equipment is functioning properly before the match begins. It is recommended to test controllers, consoles, and internet connections well in advance of the scheduled start time.

Don’t: Modifying any hardware or software that could give an unfair advantage during the match is forbidden. Players should avoid any actions that may disrupt gameplay or provide an edge over opponents.

3. Team Matches and Squad Play (4v4)

Do: Squad teams should consist of four players, with one designated as the team captain responsible for communication with tournament officials. All players should work collaboratively, employing strategies that align with the team's goals.

Don’t: Switching players mid-match or unauthorized substitutions are not allowed. Any form of team stacking, where higher-ranked players are intentionally grouped, is prohibited unless explicitly permitted by the tournament rules.

4. Solo and 1 vs. 1 Matches

Do: Solo players should be ready at their designated times and ensure they understand the rules specific to their format. In 1 vs. 1 matches, each player should demonstrate individual skill, focusing on strategic gameplay.

Don’t: Avoid delaying the start of the game. Unsportsmanlike conduct, such as taunting or excessive emoting during matches, is discouraged and may lead to penalties.

5. In-Game Communication and Streaming

Do: Communication during matches should remain professional and focused on the gameplay. Players are allowed to stream their matches, provided they have permission and comply with tournament guidelines.

Don’t: Sharing sensitive game information or strategy discussions during live streams that could be used by opponents is not allowed. Additionally, disrespectful or abusive language in voice or text chat will not be tolerated.

6. Technical Issues and Disconnections

Do: In the event of a technical issue or disconnection, immediately notify tournament officials. Matches may be paused or restarted at the discretion of the referees based on the severity of the issue.

Don’t: Falsifying technical problems to gain an advantage is unacceptable. Players should not attempt to manipulate game settings or disconnect intentionally during crucial moments.

7. Reporting Results and Disputes

Do: Teams and solo players must report their match results promptly to the tournament officials. Disputes should be raised respectfully, with evidence provided if required.

Don’t: False reporting or tampering with match results will result in disciplinary action. Arguing with officials or failing to comply with their decisions will not be tolerated.

8. Code of Conduct for Spectators and Coaches

Do: Spectators, including coaches, are encouraged to support their teams positively and respectfully. They should comply with any venue or platform-specific rules to ensure a smooth tournament experience.

Don’t: Coaching or communicating with players during live matches, unless specified as allowed, is strictly prohibited. Disruptive behavior from spectators can result in penalties for the associated team or player.
